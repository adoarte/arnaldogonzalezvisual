>> ARNALDO DRÉS GONZÁLEZ´S COLLECTIBLE SPATIAL IMAGINARY, FEATURING ANALOG FORMS INTO DIGITAL ENVIROMENTS.



>> MIXED MEDIA DRAWING AND PAINTING INSTALLATIONS + VIDEO AND PERFORMANCE ART. 


Arnaldo Drés González aka ADØ ZÁLEZ is a Venezuelan visual artist who lives and works in Hamburg, Germany since 2014. His practice explores body languages through fine arts and new media, using found objects and papers in his drawing and painting series as well as digital video, photography and sound for his audiovisual experiments. Currently he maintains the use of analogous forms within digital art in the cryptographic art scene.

Arnaldo, who began researching the contemplation of our internal environment and the poetic of our home through video art, finished his Bachelor of Fine Arts from the National Experimental University of the Arts in Caracas (2011) with his video installation "My Little and Big Universe". Later he completed his master's degree at the HKS - University of Applied Sciences and Arts in Ottersberg, Germany (2016) with the title "IMPULS", and since then he has focused on giving poetic value to every element found around him, connecting his migrant perspective that is integrated into a new environment.

Arnaldo Drés González alias ADØ ZÁLEZ es un artista visual venezolano que vive y trabaja en Hamburgo, Alemania desde 2014. Su práctica explora los lenguajes corporales a través de las bellas artes y los nuevos medios, utilizando objetos y papeles encontrados en sus series de dibujo y pintura, así como el video digital, fotografía y sonido para sus experimentos audiovisuales. Actualmente mantiene el uso de formas análogas dentro del arte digital en la escena del arte criptográfico.

Arnaldo, quien comenzó investigando la contemplación de nuestro entorno interno y la poética de nuestro hogar a través del videoarte, finalizó la Licenciatura en Bellas Artes en la Universidad Nacional Experimental de las Artes de Caracas (2011) con su videoinstalación “Mi Pequeño y Gran Universo”. " Posteriormente realizó su maestría en la HKS - Universidad de Artes y Ciencias Aplicadas en Ottersberg, Alemania (2016) con el título “IMPULS”, y desde entonces se ha enfocado en darle valor poético a cada elemento que encuentra a su alrededor, conectando su mirada migrante que se integra en un nuevo entorno.
