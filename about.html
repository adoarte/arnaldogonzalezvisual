Arnaldo Drés González explores drawing and painting with mixed media, as well as collage, book art, photography, video and performance art. The problems of the diaspora, the strangeness and peculiarities of home and abroad in relation to his own body, his thinking, feeling and action have become an integral part of his artistic process. In this context, his works are based on visual poetry from analog and digital environments that depict the figuration and abstraction of the human body as well as the landscape in order to generate reflections about migration, identity and psychogeographic space.

González completed his studies at the University of the Arts in the Plastic Arts department in Caracas, graduating with distinction in the field of Interdisciplinary Media, 2011. In September 2016 he successfully completed his master's degree (Fine Arts), accompanied by his mentor Prof. Michael Dörner, at the University of Arts in Social Sciences in Ottersberg. Since then, his career as an artist has continued and his work has been exhibited at various international art festivals, galleries and fairs. 2015 won the Honorable Mention award at the 17th altonale in Hamburg. 2019 was selected as a guest artist for the program Städtelabor at the Performing Arts Festival Berlin. 2021 nominated to the Art Prize of the Atelierkate Lesum in Bremen. From 2015 to 2019 he worked as an artist with the Fonis Gallery in Düsseldorf, and since 2016 he and his art have been represented by the “holzhauerhamburg” gallery.

In November 2021, González was invited to exhibit his artistic work in the Emerging Artists Solo Show department of the Affordable Art Fair Hamburg.

Arnaldo Drés González was born in Caracas, Venezuela, 1986. He studied Plastic Arts at the old Instituto Universitario de estudios superiores de Artes Plásticas “Armando Reverón” with the tutoring of the Venezuelan teachers and artists María Eugenia Arria, Nan González, Zacarías García, Marcos Salazar Delfino and Jorge Domínguez Dubuc.

2012 González made his first trip abroad to the island of Tenerife (Spain) where he made contact with his paternal roots for 3 months and began his photography and performance work "Resguardo y Presencia".

2013 he participated in the last edition of the "Velada de Santa Lucía" art festival in Venezuela together the Venezuelan artist Manuel Eduardo González in the Maracaibo city. From there he was invited to exhibit his visual work for the first time in Hamburg, Germany. He was participated in a group show in the "Velada Remix" art festival created by the Venezuelan artist based in Hamburg Clemencia Labín.

In 2014 he settled in Hamburg to begin his Master of Fine Arts at the HKS - University of applied sciences and arts in Ottersberg (Hochschule für Künste im Sozialen, Ottersberg, Lower Saxony) with the tutoring of the German professor and artist Michael Dönner. In 2016 he successfully closed his master studies with the projection of his experimental film "Impuls" at the “Westwerk” art space, and in the independent cinema hall "B-Movie" in Hamburg.

That same year he won the second prize at the Altonale festival, 17th edition, with his videoart "Volcano" and 2019 he was invited as guest artist at the Performing Arts Festival Berlin.

The migratory experience is deepened in González's work under his reflections on identity and psychogeographic space. In this context he uses the figuration and abstraction of the human body as well as the landscape to create drawing and video series.

Arnaldo González's drawings and paintings are made mainly with pencil, ballpoint pen and marker, sometimes mixed with acrylic paint and candle wax on paper and canvas.

Drawings as maps of one's own history - the Venezuelan-born artist Arnaldo González always searches for himself in his works on paper. Who am I, where do I go, how can I maintain communication with my family across national borders? The delicate, sometimes intensely colored drawings impress with their aesthetics and at the same time open up multiple levels of meaning that invite the viewer to enter into dialogue.
Anne Simone Krüger -
Producers Art Platform, Hamburg, 2020

Arnaldo González's works have been exhibited at international art festivals and fairs such as Milentor Gallery, Affordable Art Fair (Hamburg) and Sikka Art Fair (Dubai). 

From 2015 to 2019 he worked with the Fonis Galerie in Düsseldorf as artist, and since 2016 his art have been represented by the galerie holzhauerhamburg.

In 2021 was nominated to the Art Prize of the Atelierkate Lesum in Bremen and invited to exhibit his artistic work in the Emerging Artists Solo Show department of the Affordable Art Fair Hamburg.
